# Controlled Vocabulary

## Operational symptom

A problem experienced by the organization that may indicate a deeper structural cause.

## Organizational knowledge

Information, judgment, rules, routines, and context required for the organization to operate reliably.

## Knowledge carrier

A person, document, system, routine, prompt, AI conversation, or other medium that holds operationally relevant knowledge.

## Operational judgment

Context-sensitive knowledge used to make decisions, interpret exceptions, prioritize work, and resolve ambiguity.

## Knowledge concentration

A condition in which critical knowledge or judgment depends excessively on one carrier.

## Knowledge fragmentation

A condition in which relevant knowledge is distributed across disconnected or conflicting carriers.

## Operational fragility

Dependence on vigilance, memory, workarounds, or specific individuals to prevent failure.

## Diagnostic

A vendor-neutral process that identifies symptoms, underlying causes, dependencies, risks, and improvement priorities.

## Qualification

Interpretation of diagnostic and conversational evidence to determine likely fit, implementation need, timing, and next action.

## Translation

Conversion of internal analytical language into clear client-recognizable language without losing accuracy.

## Intelligence

Structured evidence accumulated across assessments, engagements, outcomes, research, and observed patterns.

## Pattern

A recurring relationship between symptoms, causes, conditions, interventions, or outcomes.

## Heuristic

A provisional decision aid supported by repeated evidence but not treated as an invariant.

## Canonical asset

The single authoritative source for a reusable concept, rule, prompt, template, or method.

## Generated artifact

A derivative output compiled from canonical source. It is not edited directly.

## Hidden manual coordination

Work that appears automatic or routine but actually depends on a person repeatedly checking, reconciling, reminding, translating, or correcting.

## Repeated correction loop

A failure pattern where the same issue is fixed after it occurs instead of prevented by a rule, handoff, source of truth, or ownership change.

## Durable operational knowledge

Knowledge that remains usable when a specific person is unavailable because it has an owner, location, update rule, and use context.

## Small intervention

A reversible change that reduces one real operational dependency, repeated error, or handoff problem without requiring a large transformation project.
