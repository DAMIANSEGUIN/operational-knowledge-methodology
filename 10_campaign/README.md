# Campaign System

## Objective

Generate accurate operational conversations with qualified organizations.

## Not the objective

- impressions;
- follower growth;
- generic awareness;
- selling AI;
- mass outreach.

## Primary sequence

1. Find active communities and organizations.
2. Identify recognizable operational pain.
3. Contribute relevant value.
4. Offer the vendor-neutral diagnostic.
5. Receive the response.
6. Qualify the opportunity.
7. Offer a review only when useful.

## Initial success metric

Meaningful conversations that reveal real operational conditions.

## Suitable Signals

Look for organizations where public or conversational signals suggest:

- hiring for administrative or operations relief;
- founder bottleneck;
- service delivery inconsistency;
- scattered tools;
- repeated customer or internal handoff issues;
- AI curiosity without shared practice;
- office manager overload;
- growth creating coordination strain.

## Outreach Rules

- Contribute something useful before asking for attention.
- Do not diagnose from the outside as fact.
- Ask whether the problem is real for them.
- Offer a short diagnostic, not a sales call.
- Do not use fear-based AI messaging.
- Track response, evidence, and next action.

## Opening Message Pattern

"I noticed [observable signal]. In small teams, that sometimes means [plain operational friction], but I would not assume that from the outside. If it is useful, I can share a short diagnostic checklist for finding where the work is actually getting stuck."

## Tracking Fields

- organization;
- source;
- observed signal;
- message used;
- response;
- evidence supplied;
- qualification status;
- next action;
- learning.
