## Executive Conclusion

- Methodology change required: yes
- Degree of change: strengthen and operationalize, not replace
- Highest-risk assumption: that recurring friction is usually knowledge fragility; this must remain a hypothesis until supported by client evidence
- Highest-value retained capability: practical operations support framed around problems clients already recognize
- Approval condition: use the strategic review and Field Guide v0.1 brief to build a controlled methodology, while avoiding unsupported public claims

The source material supports a methodology centered on operational symptoms, hidden knowledge dependencies, operational judgment, and small practical interventions. It does not yet supply case evidence, finished diagnostics, qualification rules, implementation templates, or outreach assets. Those must be created as controlled operating assets and tested in real engagements.

## Cross-Cutting Gaps

### Positioning

The repo must consistently say: Operations Support helps small organizations remove operational friction by making knowledge and judgment more durable. AI may help, but it is not the offer.

### Diagnostic

The diagnostic needs a stable structure: symptom, evidence, carrier, fragility, risk, intervention, confidence, human judgment.

### Qualification

The methodology needs explicit disqualifiers for prospects who want custom software, enterprise transformation, vague AI adoption, or work without access to operational evidence.

### Engagement Model

The field guide needs a weekly improvement loop compatible with roughly three hours per week and small retained-client work.

### Deliverables

Every deliverable should leave behind durable operational knowledge: map, rule, checklist, template, review cadence, prompt, or decision record.

### Marketing

Marketing should offer a practical diagnostic, not promise transformation or lead with framework language.

### Analytics

Track conversations, diagnostic patterns, interventions, outcomes, and corrections. Do not publish benchmarks until evidence exists.

### Learning

Explained disagreement by Damian is a primary learning source and must be captured in learning records.

## Proposed Target Architecture

1. Orientation explains the repository and client-facing promise.
2. Strategic review defines why the method exists.
3. Gap analysis authorizes controlled methodology construction.
4. Methodology defines the core operating method.
5. Field guide converts method into field sequence.
6. Diagnostic, qualification, translation, implementation, intelligence, and campaign modules execute the method.
7. Agents, prompts, and templates make the work repeatable.
8. Governance and validation prevent drift.
