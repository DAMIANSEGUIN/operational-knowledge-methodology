# Translation System

## Purpose

Convert rigorous internal analysis into language a busy owner or office manager recognizes immediately.

## Rules

- Use client language.
- Describe observable effects.
- Do not lead with architecture.
- Do not lead with AI.
- One idea per sentence.
- Avoid consultant terminology.
- Preserve uncertainty.
- Never exaggerate evidence.
- The client should understand the point in under fifteen seconds.

## Example

Internal:

Critical operational judgment is concentrated in one undocumented human carrier.

External:

A lot of the office appears to depend on one person remembering how everything works.

## Translation Pattern

Use this sequence:

1. Name the visible problem.
2. Explain the likely cause without jargon.
3. State the practical risk.
4. Recommend the smallest next step.
5. Preserve uncertainty.

Example:

- Internal: "Knowledge fragmentation across disconnected systems creates repeated manual reconciliation."
- External: "The same information is living in too many places, so someone has to keep checking which version is right."

## Forbidden Substitutions

- Do not say "knowledge architecture" to a client unless they already use that language.
- Do not say "governance" when "shared rule" is enough.
- Do not say "AI transformation" when the work is "people are using AI differently and no one knows what is safe or useful."
- Do not promise savings, growth, or efficiency percentages without evidence.

## Standard Client Finding

1. What we saw.
2. Why it matters.
3. What is probably causing it.
4. What we do not know yet.
5. The smallest useful fix.
6. What would show that the fix worked.
