# Diagnostic System

## Purpose

Identify the relationship between observable operational symptoms and the organization's underlying knowledge carriers, judgment dependencies, systems, routines, and adaptation capacity.

## Non-commercial constraint

The diagnostic must not recommend hiring Operations Support.

## Required outputs

- operational symptom map;
- knowledge carrier map;
- judgment concentration map;
- fragmentation risks;
- workflow consequences;
- AI-use consistency assessment;
- prioritized improvements;
- DIY, guided, or delegated implementation paths;
- missing information;
- confidence levels;
- evidence statements.

## Diagnostic Model

Each finding uses this structure:

1. Symptom: what the organization experiences.
2. Evidence: what was observed or supplied.
3. Carrier: where the relevant knowledge or judgment currently lives.
4. Fragility: concentration, fragmentation, tacit judgment, repeated correction, manual translation, tool mismatch, AI instability, or missing ownership.
5. Consequence: delay, risk, repeated work, inconsistency, dependence, cost, or stress.
6. Intervention: smallest useful change.
7. Confidence: evidence level and uncertainty.
8. Human judgment: what Damian or the client must decide.

## Question Sequence

1. What keeps recurring even after people fix it?
2. What slows down when one person is away?
3. Which decisions require someone to "just know" the context?
4. Where does the same information get copied, checked, or reconciled?
5. Which tools hold conflicting versions of the truth?
6. What exceptions are handled differently by different people?
7. What does a new person struggle to learn?
8. Where is AI being used, and how do people review the output?
9. Who owns the process, the data, the rule, and the update?
10. What small change would create visible relief first?

## Prioritization

Score each candidate issue from 1 to 3:

- pain: how often or severely the issue is felt;
- fragility: how dependent it is on memory, one person, or hidden judgment;
- feasibility: how small and reversible the first intervention can be;
- learning value: how much the intervention will reveal.

Prioritize high pain, high fragility, and high feasibility. Do not choose a large project when a smaller proof can reduce the same risk.

## Failure Modes

- accepting a stakeholder complaint as a diagnosis;
- recommending software before mapping the workflow;
- ignoring the person carrying the work;
- treating AI inconsistency as only a policy issue;
- missing informal workarounds that currently prevent failure;
- overstating confidence when evidence is weak.
