## Executive Brief

This Field Guide is the operational doctrine for the Operations Support business.

It is written for an AI implementation team working alongside Damian Seguin.

The objective is to identify organizations whose operational problems are symptoms of fragile organizational knowledge, diagnose the underlying causes, recommend practical interventions, and support implementation.

Clients do not buy AI.

Clients do not buy governance.

Clients buy relief from recurring operational problems.

Typical questions include:

- Why does everything depend on one person?
- Why are we constantly fixing the same mistakes?
- Why are our processes inconsistent?
- Why does work slow down whenever someone is away?
- Why do we have five systems that do not talk to each other?
- Why does every employee use AI differently?

These are treated as observable symptoms rather than independent problems.

The working hypothesis is that recurring operational friction often reflects weaknesses in the organization's knowledge architecture and operational judgment.

AI is treated as an implementation capability, not the product.

## Primary Objectives

1. Reveal hidden operational knowledge dependencies.
2. Externalize critical operational judgment.
3. Reduce operational fragility.
4. Improve organizational adaptability.
5. Leave every client with more durable, transferable operational knowledge.
