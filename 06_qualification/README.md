# Qualification System

## Purpose

Interpret diagnostic results and lead responses to determine fit, need, timing, trust, implementation feasibility, and the next best action.

## AI responsibility

Produce a decision package.

## Human responsibility

Approve or revise judgments involving fit, messaging, trust, timing, pricing, and commitments.

## Possible outcomes

- Continue value-first conversation
- Ask targeted questions
- Offer review meeting
- DIY recommendation
- Guided implementation
- Fractional Operations Support
- Refer elsewhere
- No action

## Fit Criteria

Good fit requires evidence of at least three:

- recurring operational friction;
- knowledge concentrated in one or two people;
- repeated correction loops;
- undocumented judgment;
- disconnected tools or handoffs;
- leadership willing to inspect the actual work;
- small improvement would create visible relief;
- existing team can participate without a large transformation project.

## Disqualifiers

Disqualify or refer when:

- the prospect mainly wants custom software development;
- the prospect wants AI adoption without operational diagnosis;
- no one with authority will engage;
- the problem is primarily legal, financial, clinical, or HR-sensitive beyond scope;
- the expected outcome is a guaranteed business result;
- the organization refuses to provide examples or artifacts;
- the work requires enterprise-scale change management.

## Decision Rules

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| Clear pain, enough access, small first intervention | offer diagnostic review or small engagement |
| Clear pain but weak access | ask targeted evidence questions |
| Weak pain but strong relationship | continue value-first conversation |
| Strong need but outside scope | refer or recommend another provider |
| Unclear authority or trust | do not sell; clarify conditions |

## Qualification Output

- decision;
- evidence;
- missing information;
- fit strengths;
- fit risks;
- recommended next action;
- human approval required.
