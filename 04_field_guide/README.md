# Field Guide

Canonical scope: the human-usable sequence from first signal to measured improvement.

## Operating Sequence

| State | Purpose | Output | Human judgment |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Identify | Notice organizations with recognizable operational friction | lead note | whether signal is worth attention |
| 2. Research | Gather public and supplied context | context brief | whether outreach is appropriate |
| 3. Calibrate message | Translate the likely problem into plain language | opening message | tone, timing, trust |
| 4. Begin conversation | Ask about the experienced problem, not the method | conversation record | whether to continue |
| 5. Offer diagnostic | Offer a vendor-neutral review of the operational issue | diagnostic invitation | whether offer fits relationship |
| 6. Gather context | Collect examples, artifacts, workflow notes, and constraints | evidence log | access and sensitivity |
| 7. Run diagnostic | Map symptoms to knowledge carriers, fragility, and risks | diagnostic findings | whether findings are fair |
| 8. Qualify | Decide fit, timing, authority, and commercial viability | qualification decision | fit and commitment |
| 9. Present findings | Explain the problem in client language | findings report | claims and recommendations |
| 10. Select intervention | Choose one small useful improvement | implementation plan | scope and priority |
| 11. Implement | Create or adjust the asset, handoff, rule, or routine | operational asset | acceptance of change |
| 12. Measure | Compare before and after | outcome review | whether result matters |
| 13. Continue or close | Retain, refer, or end cleanly | next-action decision | relationship and pricing |
| 14. Capture learning | Record corrections, outcomes, and reusable patterns | learning record | methodology update approval |

## Field Rules

- Lead with the problem the client already feels.
- Ask for examples before explaining causes.
- Treat every claim as provisional until supported by evidence.
- Prefer existing people and tools before new software.
- Choose small reversible changes.
- Leave behind a durable asset after every implementation.
- Capture disagreement and correction.

## Minimum Diagnostic Invitation

"If useful, I can look at where this work is getting stuck and give you a short map of what is causing the repeated friction, what depends on one person, and what small change would reduce the pressure first."

## Completion Criteria

The field guide is working when a new operator can move from a lead signal to a diagnostic, qualification decision, implementation plan, outcome review, and learning record without relying on conversation history.
